The Problem

Without physical barriers, cars treat bike lanes as parking spots. This forces riders to swerve into 45mph traffic.

LIVE VIEW: Notice the grey cars parked in the bike lanes on both sides. Riders are forced to merge dangerously.

The Data

Evidence from cities like NYC and Seattle shows that separation saves lives.

+15% Traffic Efficiency
-40% Injury Risk

Success: Tech Parkway

A proven model near Georgia Tech. When we separate traffic, everyone wins.

LIVE VIEW: The barriers have risen. The lanes turn green. Riders now have a dedicated, safe path.

The Solution

Extending this protection to Marietta Street.

  • Raised Lanes: Distinct from road grade.
  • Bollards: Prevent car entry.
  • No Parking: Physical inability to block lanes.
  • Clear Signage: Predictable merging.

Community Benefits

  • Safety: Fewer ER visits for students.
  • Traffic: Less chaotic merging for drivers.
  • Air: Reduced emissions from idling.
